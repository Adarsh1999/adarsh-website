About

From a young age, I've been captivated by the world of software development. I would spend countless hours coding and bringing my ideas to life using languages like C++, Java, and Python. It all started with simple "Hello World" programs, but soon I found myself diving into bigger projects and even launching my own startup. This unwavering passion for coding led me to pursue a Bachelor's and then a Master's degree in Software Engineering.

I've had the opportunity to dive into various technologies, always taking a hands-on, project-based approach to learn something new. Working in startups, both as an intern and full-time, has been a stroke of luck. It's incredible how much I've learned and grown during my time there. The best part about working in a startup is that you get to immerse yourself in the entire software developer lifecycle. You become a coding wizard, juggling different aspects of the project and gaining knowledge in almost every part. It's a thrilling experience that fuels continuous learning and growth.

This knowledge helped me to write code for my own startup adventure known as ATRIS which is AI-based personal notes and Minutes of Meetings automation platform. I have also written a research paper on Architectural Insight of Neural Information Extraction, Retrieval, and Processing for Multimodal Neural Search and published it in Springers.

Outside of studies, I enjoy participating in hackathons and attending tech conferences to connect with other professionals in the field. I also engage in personal projects where I can explore new technologies and experiment with creative ideas.
